Your Goal Measure Results

The results being displayed are for the user:

The measure you completed was designed to assess your most important goals and how you think about these goal pursuits. A number of studies have shown that all goals are not equal when it comes to psychological well-being.

Specifically, goals that satisfy fundamental human needs are more likely to lead to well-being. If the goals you are striving for fail to meet these psychological needs, you are more likely to experience psychological distress. They are described as needs because as water and food are required for physical survival and thriving, these psychological needs are required for psychological survival and thriving.

According to self-determination theory (Ryan & Deci, 2000), humans have three essential psychological needs:

  • Competence: which is the experience of feeling effective and capable of achieving desired outcomes when pursuing/executing a goal.
  • Relatedness: which is the experience of intimacy and genuine connection with others when pursuing/executing a goal.
  • Autonomy: which is the experience of self-determination, full willingness, and volition when pursuing/executing a goal.

    • Evaluating specific individual goal feedback: When evaluating your feedback for a specific goal, consider whether that goal satisfies at least one of the three psychological needs. It isn’t necessary that a goal meet all 3 psychological needs. But if goal isn’t meeting at least one of these basic psychological needs, research suggest it is unlikely to lead to well-being and happiness.

    Evaluating overall goal feedback: When evaluating your feedback for all of your goals collectively, consider whether a particular psychological need is being neglected.

    For each goal, use the following scale to interpret your scores:

  • 4.5 and above = a very high level of need satisfaction
  • 4.0 – 4.4 = high level of need satisfaction
  • 3.5 - 3.9 = average need satisfaction
  • 2.5 – 3.4 = moderate need frustration
  • 2.0 – 2.4 = high need frustration
  • 1.0 – 1.9 = very high need frustration
