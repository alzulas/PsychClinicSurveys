Your Goal Measure Results

The results being displayed are for the user:

The measure you completed was designed to assess your self concepts. Research has shown that rather than a single self-concept we actually possess a “family of selves”, and that we experience somewhat different “selves” when with different others or when engaged in different roles or activities. Individuals vary in how similar or different these multiple self-representations are from one another.

For each self-representation, this measure assessed the extent to which basic psychological needs are being met. According to self-determination theory (Ryan & Deci, 2000), humans have three needs:

  • Competence-refers to the experience of feeling effective and capable of achieving desired outcomes.
  • Relatedness-refers to the experience of intimacy and genuine connection with others.
  • Autonomy-refers to the experience of self-determination, full willingness, and volition when carrying out an activity.

    • Research has shown that individuals who experience higher levels of their needs being satisfied are more likely to experience higher levels of psychological functioning and well-being. In contrast, individuals who experience frustration of these needs are more likely to experience distress.

    To the extent that each of you experience high need satisfaction when these different selves are active, you are likely to experience greater psychological well-being. In this sense, your self-concept is adaptive, strong.

    For each need, use the following scale to interpret your scores:

  • 4.5 and above = a very high level of need satisfaction
  • 4.0 – 4.4 = high level of need satisfaction
  • 3.5 - 3.9 = average need satisfaction
  • 2.5 – 3.4 = moderate need frustration
  • 2.0 – 2.4 = high need frustration
  • 1.0 – 1.9 = very high need frustration
